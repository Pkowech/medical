Title of the course

PPB 422: DERMATOLOGICAL AND OCULAR DISEASES

(35 Contact hours)

Prerequisites required:

  1. ALL HMP Courses in Level I and II
  2. ALL HMA Courses in Level I and II
  3. Purpose of the course

The aim of the course is to equip students with knowledge, skills and attitudes on the pharmacology of drugs used for skin and eye conditions/disorders.

  1. Expected Learning Outcomes

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:

  1. Understand the anatomy of the skin and its appendages
  2. Understand the physiology of the skin and its appendages
  3. Gain competencies that will enable you to identify dermatological diseases/disorders
  4. Understand the mechanisms of action, therapeutic uses, and toxicities of topical and systemic drugs used to treat dermatological diseases /disorders
  5. Gain competencies that will enable you to identify ocular diseases
  6. Understand the mechanisms of action, therapeutic uses, and toxicities of topical and systemic drugs used to treat ocular disorders.
  7. Acquire knowledge and skills necessary for management of dermatological disorders
  8. Develop interpersonal skills necessary for management of ocular disorders.
